# Aetna Clinical Policy Bulletin: Ocrelizumab / Multiple Sclerosis (CPB #0264)
# Source: https://www.aetna.com/cpb/medical/data/200_299/0264.html
# Retrieved: 2026-04-04

## Policy Number
0264 - Multiple Sclerosis

## Precertification Requirement
Precertification of multiple sclerosis medications (Briumvi, Lemtrada, Ocrevus, Ocrevus Zunovo, Tyruko, Tysabri) are required for all applicable plan designs.

## Approved Indications

Aetna considers ocrelizumab medically necessary for three conditions:

1. **Relapsing Forms of MS** - Members diagnosed with relapsing-remitting or secondary progressive disease experiencing continued relapses
2. **Clinically Isolated Syndrome (CIS)** - Treatment of initial demyelinating episode
3. **Primary Progressive MS (PPMS)** - Treatment of progressive disease from onset

## Initial Approval Criteria
Members must have documented diagnosis of one eligible MS phenotype. No specific trial-and-error requirements are detailed for ocrelizumab's initial use.

## Continuation of Therapy
Coverage continues for members "who achieve or maintain a positive clinical response as evidenced by experiencing disease stability or improvement" during treatment.

## Concomitant Use Restrictions
Members cannot use ocrelizumab simultaneously with other disease-modifying MS agents (excluding Ampyra and Nuedexta, which are non-disease-modifying).

## Pediatric Authorization
"Authorization may be granted for pediatric members less than 18 years of age when benefits outweigh risks."

## Dosing (Intravenous Formulation)
- Initial: 300 mg infusion, repeated two weeks later
- Maintenance: Single 600 mg infusion every 6 months
- Pre-treatment screening required for Hepatitis B and serum quantitative immunoglobulins.

## Dosing (Subcutaneous Formulation - Ocrevus Zunovo)
920 mg ocrelizumab with hyaluronidase administered as single 23 mL subcutaneous injection in abdomen every 6 months.

## Exclusions
Aetna considers concomitant use with other disease-modifying therapies "experimental, investigational, or unproven."

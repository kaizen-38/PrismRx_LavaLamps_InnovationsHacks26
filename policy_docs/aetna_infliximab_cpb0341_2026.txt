# Aetna Clinical Policy Bulletin: Infliximab (CPB #0341)
# Source: https://www.aetna.com/cpb/medical/data/300_399/0341.html
# Retrieved: 2026-04-04

## Policy Overview
Aetna covers infliximab products (Remicade, Renflexis, Avsola, Inflectra, Zymfentra) for commercial medical plans when medically necessary and criteria are met. All products require precertification.

## Covered Indications

**Gastrointestinal:**
- Moderately to severely active Crohn's disease
- Moderately to severely active ulcerative colitis

**Rheumatologic (Avsola/Inflectra/infliximab/Remicade/Renflexis only):**
- Rheumatoid arthritis (with prior biologic/targeted synthetic drug use OR specific biomarker and disease activity criteria)
- Ankylosing spondylitis and non-radiographic axial spondyloarthritis
- Psoriatic arthritis
- Reactive arthritis

**Dermatologic:**
- Plaque psoriasis (moderate to severe)
- Hidradenitis suppurativa (severe, refractory)
- Pyoderma gangrenosum

**Other Conditions:**
- Behçet's disease
- Sarcoidosis
- Takayasu's arteritis
- Uveitis
- Immune checkpoint inhibitor-related toxicity
- Acute graft versus host disease

## Prescriber Requirements
Coverage requires prescription by or consultation with specialty-appropriate providers (gastroenterologists, rheumatologists, dermatologists, ophthalmologists, oncologists, or hematologists depending on indication).

## Key Authorization Requirements

**TB Testing:** Negative tuberculosis test (TST or IGRA) within 12 months before initiating therapy for biologic-naive patients.

**Drug Combination Restrictions:** Cannot use concomitantly with other biologics or targeted synthetic drugs for the same indication.

**Continuation Criteria:** Members must demonstrate positive clinical response (remission, low disease activity, or measurable improvement in disease markers).

## Dosing Summary
- **Standard doses:** 3-5 mg/kg IV at weeks 0, 2, 6, then maintenance every 6-8 weeks
- **Zymfentra (SC):** 120 mg weekly after IV induction completion
- Dosing subject to FDA-approved labeling and specialty medication quantity limits

## Experimental/Unproven Uses
The bulletin designates numerous conditions as unproven, including: acne fulminans, asthma, celiac disease, COVID-19 pneumonia, granuloma annulare, IgG4-related disease, microscopic colitis, polymyositis, scleroderma, and Sjögren's syndrome.
